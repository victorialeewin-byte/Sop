Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Characterization of Berberine (BBR) Nanomicelles in Intestinal Fluid (IF)

Document ID: SOP-LAB-042

Effective Date: June 8, 2026

1. Objective

To establish an optimized instrument protocol using a 190 nm latex standard and to evaluate the particle size distribution and formation of Berberine (BBR) nanomicelles (target range: 10 nm – 220 nm) within simulated Intestinal Fluid (IF).

2. Materials and Reagents

  • Analyte: Berberine (BBR) stock solution.
  • Matrix: Simulated Intestinal Fluid (IF), pre-filtered through a 0.22 µm membrane.
  • Standard: Latex beads (Mean Diameter = 190 nm, original concentration = 1.0 x 1013 particles/mL).
  • Diluent: Ultrapure Water (H2O), pre-filtered (0.02 µm or 0.1 µm) to remove background particulates.

3. Standard Serial Dilution Protocol

To achieve optimal instrument tracking and avoid camera saturation, the original latex stock must be diluted to an optimal working concentration of approximately 1.0 x 108 particles/mL (a 100,000-fold dilution).

  1. Step 1 (1:100): Transfer 10 µL of the Latex Stock into 990 µL of H2O. Vortex thoroughly.
    Yields: 1.0 x 1011 particles/mL.
  2. Step 2 (1:100): Transfer 10 µL of the Step 1 solution into 990 µL of H2O. Vortex thoroughly.
    Yields: 1.0 x 109 particles/mL.
  3. Step 3 (1:10): Transfer 100 µL of the Step 2 solution into 900 µL of H2O. Vortex thoroughly.
    Yields: 1.0 x 108 particles/mL (Final Working Standard).

4. Instrument Optimization (Parameter Setting)

  1. Flush System: Prime the instrument chamber with H2O to ensure a clean baseline.
  2. Standard Calibration (H2O + Latex):
    • Inject the Step 3 Latex Working Standard (1.0 x 108 particles/mL) into the sample chamber.
    • Adjust the focus until the 190 nm particles appear as sharp, distinct point-scatterers.
    • Optimize the Camera Level (Exposure Time) and Gain. Ensure the settings are high enough to track the 190 nm particles continuously, but low enough to prevent pixel blooming (oversaturation).
CRITICAL NOTE: Lock these specific instrument settings (Exposure/Gain) for all subsequent matrix cross-checks and experimental samples to maintain operational consistency.

5. Matrix Validation and Experimental Groups

Evaluate the samples in the following sequential order to validate the matrix and accurately measure the experimental drug formulation:

Group & Name Procedure Purpose & Expected Outcome
Group 1:
Blank Matrix		 Inject the filtered intestinal fluid (IF) alone. Establishes background noise and the baseline particle profile of the complex biological fluid. Expect a low-intensity, broad peak (or no peak if highly pure).
Group 2:
Positive Control		 Spike the IF with the Step 2 latex solution to achieve a final concentration of ~1.0 x 108 particles/mL in the IF matrix. Inject and measure. Cross-checks and confirms that the predefined camera and gain settings can accurately resolve the distinct 190 nm peak despite the refractive index and complex matrix of the intestinal fluid.
Group 3:
Experimental Sample		 Prepare the BBR formulation in IF according to the defined concentration gradient. Incubate if necessary to allow micelle self-assembly. Inject into the chamber. Allows direct observation and characterization of the spontaneous formation of BBR nanomicelles.

6. Data Acquisition and Analysis

  1. Capture a minimum of 5 video recordings (e.g., 30–60 seconds each) per sample to ensure robust statistical reliability.
  2. Process the captures using the tracking software to generate the size distribution histogram (Concentration vs. Size).
  3. Interpretation Criteria:
    • Free BBR: Expected to fall below the 10 nm detection threshold (defined as < 10 nm = free).
    • Nano BBR / IF Micelles: Expected to resolve as distinct peaks within the 10 nm – 220 nm range.
  4. Export the mean size, mode size, D10, D50, D90, and total particle concentration metrics for comprehensive reporting.
